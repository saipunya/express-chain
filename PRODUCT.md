# Product

## Register

product

## Users

CoopChain serves staff of the Chaiyaphum Provincial Cooperative Office, cooperatives, and farmer groups. Users work in an operational government context: checking institutional records, following deadlines, preparing reports, managing meeting rooms and travel workflows, and accessing services from desktop and mobile devices during daily office work.

## Product Purpose

CoopChain centralizes cooperative-office information and service workflows for Chaiyaphum. It helps staff and member institutions find records, track important dates, manage documents, coordinate internal services, and run specialized route-based subsystems when a workflow needs a dedicated surface. Success means fewer scattered spreadsheets, clearer task status, faster lookup, and dependable screens that support repeated public-sector work.

## Brand Personality

Modern public service, trustworthy, easy to use. The interface should feel official without being dated, calm without being dull, and practical without losing polish. Copy should be direct, respectful, and action-oriented.

## Anti-references

Avoid loud marketing pages, over-decorated dashboards, heavy animation, low-contrast gray text, color palettes that feel playful rather than official, and layouts that prioritize visual spectacle over task clarity. Avoid designs that look like generic SaaS templates when a screen is primarily a government operations tool.

## Design Principles

- Make status visible: deadlines, counts, approvals, and next actions should be easy to scan without opening multiple pages.
- Respect operational rhythm: common tasks should be reachable quickly, with predictable navigation and familiar controls.
- Modernize government work: use contemporary spacing, typography, and states while preserving the credibility expected of a public office.
- Separate focused subsystems cleanly: route-specific tools can have tailored flows, but they should still feel part of CoopChain.
- Design for mixed devices: desktop density matters for staff work, while mobile layouts must remain readable and tappable.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Target at least WCAG AA in practice: readable contrast, keyboard-accessible controls, visible focus states, responsive layouts, and clear Thai labels. Respect reduced-motion preferences and avoid relying on color alone to communicate state.
